Gameplan Jobs

Gameplan Jobs offers assessments and skill mapping for high-volume recruiting. I was the Director of Product for the initial prototype, GamePlan Learning, which focused exclusively on preparing college students for their first job. It enabled our startup to enter several accelerators, including Emerge in the UK, which earned us the funding for our the current iteration.

We decided to change our focus from college students to anyone entering the workforce, but discovered that our knowledge graph also offered an opportunity for companies to groom their best employees.

As the product owner for three different scrum teams, I had exposure to both the MEAN and LAMP stacks. As a developer in the final iteration, I used Angular to make user profiles more interactive, especially in displaying scores and recommending career paths.

In Sandi Metz's book, Practical Object-Oriented Design in Ruby, she makes two observations that best represent why my experience with Gameplan made me want to transition from product management to development:

  • Development is one of the rare jobs in which being more effecient at work goes hand in hand with enjoying it.
  • Dealing with frustrating code is virtually a prerequisite for valuing cleanly written code.

Gameplan was, to date, the largest and most challenging project I have worked on in any capacity. Managing production remotely across several continents required discipline and vision, which ultimately led me to discovering my love for web development.
